INT. COFFEE SHOP - DAY JANE (30s) sits at a corner table, laptop open. She types furiously, ignoring the world around her. Sunlight streams through the window behind her. MARK (30s) approaches hesitantly from the entrance, two takeaway coffees in hand. He pauses near her table, uncertain. MARK (quietly) Mind if I join? Jane doesn't look up. Her fingers keep moving across the keyboard. JANE without looking up It's a free country. Mark sets down the coffees and takes the seat across from her. He watches her work for a moment. MARK Jane, please. We need to talk about yesterday. Jane stops typing. She looks up, her expression unreadable. JANE There's nothing to talk about. You made your choice. MARK It wasn't a choice. It was— JANE sharper It was a decision. You made it. Now live with it. She returns to her typing. Mark watches her, defeated. CUT TO: INT. JANE'S APARTMENT - NIGHT A small, sparsely furnished apartment. Boxes are stacked against one wall—semi-packed, as if she's always ready to leave but never quite does. Jane sits on the couch, laptop closed, staring at nothing. The coffee from earlier sits untouched on the table. Her phone buzzes. Once. Twice. She ignores it.
